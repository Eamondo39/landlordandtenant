Key Areas of Property Law Relevant to Landlord & Tenant Surveying

Understanding the legal framework that underpins commercial landlord and tenant relationships is essential for property professionals and anyone involved in managing, owning, or occupying commercial premises...

1. Law of Property Act 1925 – Service of Notices and Forfeiture

Section 62: Certain rights—such as easements—can automatically pass with the sale or grant of a lease. Landlords often exclude s.62 to avoid unintentionally granting additional rights.

Section 146: Governs service of forfeiture notices for breaches other than non-payment of rent...

Section 147: Deals with dilapidations and tenant relief in certain circumstances...

Section 196: Notices are valid if left at tenant’s last known residence/business. Recorded delivery is recommended.

2. Landlord and Tenant Act 1927 – Alterations, Improvements and Dilapidations

Section 18: Caps terminal dilapidations claims to diminution in value...

Section 3: Tenant improvements require notice to the landlord; landlords have two months to respond or tenant may claim compensation...

Section 19(1)(a): Qualified covenants interpreted as fully qualified covenants; landlords cannot unreasonably withhold consent.

3. Landlord and Tenant Act 1954 – Security of Tenure and Lease Renewals

Sections 24–31A govern renewal, termination, and new tenancy terms...

4. Landlord and Tenant Act 1988 – Assignments and Reasonableness

Landlords must act reasonably when considering tenant requests for assignment, underletting, or other consents...

5. Landlord and Tenant (Covenants) Act 1995 – Post-1996 Leases

This Act abolished privity of contract for leases granted after 1 January 1996...

6. Leasehold Property Repairs Act 1938 – Restrictions on Enforcement of Repairs

Restricts landlord forfeiture for disrepair where leases are seven years or more and more than three years remain...
